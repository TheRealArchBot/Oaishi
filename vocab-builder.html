🏃‍♀️ 1. Action Verbs (What is happening?)

Instead of saying...Try using...Example in a sentence
Show / ProveDemonstrate / IllustrateStudies demonstrate that exercise reduces stress.
Make / CreateGenerate / ProduceThis policy will generate new opportunities.
Fix / SolveResolve / RemedyWe must resolve the systemic issues immediately.
Help / SupportFacilitate / BolsterGood lighting facilitates better concentration.
Stop / PreventHinder / InhibitA lack of funding will hinder our progress.
ChangeTransform / AlterThe internet has transformed global communication.
Think aboutConsider / AnalyzeWe must analyze the long-term effects.
Give / Hand outAllocate / DistributeThe government must allocate resources fairly.
Find / DiscoverIdentify / AscertainResearchers identified the root cause of the problem.
Keep / SavePreserve / RetainIt is important to preserve historical monuments.
ExplainClarify / ElucidateThe professor elucidated the complex theory.
Look atExamine / InvestigateThe committee will investigate the financial records.
Put inIncorporate / IntegrateWe should incorporate more technology into schools.
Say / StateAssert / ArticulateThe author asserts that climate change is urgent.
Start / BeginInitiate / CommenceThe project will commence next week.
UseUtilize / EmployCompanies utilize data to target ads.
NeedRequire / NecessitateRapid growth necessitates new infrastructure.

Good / ImportantCrucial / VitalEducation is crucial for national development.
Big / HugeSubstantial / ProfoundAI has caused a profound shift in the industry.
Small / TinyMarginal / NegligibleThe new tax caused only a marginal increase in prices.
Clear / Easy to seeEvident / ApparentIt is evident that climate change is worsening.

PoorImpoverished / DisadvantagedImpoverished communities suffer the most.
Fast / QuickRapid / AcceleratedThe rapid growth of the city caused traffic issues.

DifferentDistinct / DiverseThe country is known for its diverse culture.
EnoughSufficient / AdequateThe hospital lacks sufficient medical supplies.
NewInnovative / NovelThis startup proposed an innovative solution.
EmptyVacant / VoidThe building remained vacant for years.

🔗 3. Connecting Words (Glue your sentences together!)

Instead of saying...Try using...When to use it
Also / AndFurthermore / MoreoverWhen adding another point to support your argument.
But / HoweverNevertheless / ConverselyWhen showing an opposite idea or a plot twist.
Because / SoConsequently / ThereforeWhen showing the result of something happening.
For exampleFor instance / NamelyWhen giving specific proof to back up your claim.
In conclusionUltimately / To summarizeWhen wrapping up your final paragraph.
In shortBriefly / EssentiallyWhen giving the "TL;DR" version of a long thought.
For sureUndoubtedly / CertainlyWhen you are 100% confident in a claim.
Because ofDue to / As a consequence ofWhen pointing at the direct cause of a problem.
BasicallyFundamentally / PrimarilyWhen explaining the core root of an issue.
